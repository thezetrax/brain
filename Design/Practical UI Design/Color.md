# Contrast

- Contrast is the measure of brightness between two colors
- Contrast is expressed as a ratio X:Y
- The measure of contrast ranges from 1:1 to 21:1, 1:1 being lowest contrast & 21:1 being highest contrast available

## Tips on Minimum Contrast to Achieve

- **3:1** for **large text**
- **large text** refers to text with *18px with bold font weight* or above *24px with regular weight*
- **4.5:1** for **small text**
- **small text** refers to text with the font size **18px or smaller**

## Improved Contrast Measurement

- **Accessible Perceptual Contrast Algorithm (APCA)** is a new/improved contrast measurement method
- **APCA** is a part of **WCAG 3** draft, this new standard for measuring contrast
- **APCA** helps resolve the existing limitations with **WCAG 2**

### Measurement using APCA

- Contrast is measured in numbers
- Higher number is equivalent to higher contrast
- Contrast value also depends on *text size and weight*
- Smaller and thinner text results in lower score

### **APCA** contrast guideline

- 90: preferred for body text (14px regular & above)
- 75: minimum for body text (18px regular & above)
- 60: minimum for other text (*24px regular or 16px bold* & above)
- 45: minimum for large text (*36px regular or 24px bold* & above)
- 30: absolute minimum for **text in form fields placehoder text and disabled button text**
- 15: minimum for non-text elements
